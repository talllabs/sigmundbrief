Solopreneurs, with AI support, rival mid-sized firms

AI is transforming the business landscape by enabling solopreneurs to compete with mid-sized companies, with 30.4 million solopreneurs generating $1.75 trillion in output. AI handles administrative tasks, enhances sales and marketing and ensures compliance, allowing individuals to focus on their expertise. This shift is leading to a decline in mid-sized companies and the rise of a new model of work driven by AI-powered individuals, writes Andy Kurtzig, the CEO of Pearl.

Smart Funding

Constant fundraising no substitute for a solid business

(Thanakorn Lappattaranan/Getty Images)

startup founders who depend on constant fundraising often mask fundamental weaknesses in their business operations, writes . SetSchedule CEO Roy Dekel, who shares his experience of repeatedly turning to investors whenever problems arose, noting that this influx of capital delayed tough decisions and covered up flaws in the company's model. Ultimately, Dekel argues that if a business cannot survive on its own revenue and continually needs external funding to stay afloat, it is not a sustainable venture but rather just a series of fundraising pitches.

Full Story: Inc. (tiered subscription model) (2/23)

Awards are not essential for VC in longevity investing

Awards are not essential for startups to attract venture capital, writes Allen Law, an investor in the longevity and healthspan sector, who emphasizes that investors prioritize alignment and evidence over recognition. Law notes that awards can boost visibility, but he says they are not reliable indicators of long-term success. Law advises startups to focus on building trust through rigorous evidence and measurable impact rather than pursuing awards for credibility.

Tips for business owners to prepare for future exit

Entrepreneurs can prepare for a business exit from the start, with financial planner Jamie Carroll advising business owners to maintain transparent financials, align legal structures with personal goals, scale the business independently of the founder, protect intellectual property and plan for long-term objectives.

Full Story: Kiplinger (3/2)

When it comes to getting a mentor, don't shoot for the top

If you're looking for a mentor to help you advance your career, aiming too high for an "aspirational mentor" can be discouraging, says Bill Gurley, a general partner at Benchmark, who suggests finding someone who is two steps below where you want to be, as they may be more amenable to helping you. "Discover somebody -- tools like LinkedIn make this so easy -- and be the first person to ever call them and ask them to be a mentor, because they'll be flattered. You're going to have way more success with that interaction than shooting high," says Gurley.

Smart Living

Be in nature more to create a sense of calm

(Johner Images/Getty Images)

There is mental health power in spending time with natural elements, and it doesn't have to involve strenuous activities such as hiking, say licensed clinical social workers Erin Kehrier, Sarah Panzer and Laura Sgro. Examples include streaming nature sounds, doing some light gardening or incorporating nature images into a meditation session. "Being with nature, in one of its many forms, can help people feel more grounded and less overwhelmed. It can offer a sense of calm and help the body settle," says Kehrier.
